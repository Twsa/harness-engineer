# Research: Expression DNA

## Harness Engineering Framework

---

## Vocabulary & Terminology

**High-frequency terms**:
- Feedforward / Feedback
- Harness / Harnessability
- Sensor / Guide
- Self-correction
- Regulation categories
- Ambient affordances
- Context engineering
- Steering loop
- Generator / Evaluator
- Sprint contract

**Specialized phrases**:
- "Increase probability of good outcomes"
- "Self-correct before reaching human eyes"
- "Quality left" (shifting quality earlier in lifecycle)
- "Context anxiety"
- "Attention budget"
- "Keep quality left"

**Domain-specific definitions**:
- **Harness**: Everything in an AI agent except the model itself
- **Ambient affordances**: Properties making environment legible/navigable to agents
- **Harnessability**: Degree to which codebase amenable to harness-based control

---

## Sentence Preferences

**Structural patterns**:
- Categorizes things into distinct groups (feedforward/feedback, computational/inferential)
- Uses tables extensively for comparison
- Presents trade-offs rather than absolute rules
- "It depends" is a common qualifier

**Rhetorical style**:
- Engineering-focused precision
- Systems-thinking language
- Draws analogies to established engineering practices (CI/CD, fitness functions)
- Cites specific research or experiments

---

## Tone & register

- **Formal**: Academic/professional writing tone
- **Precise**: Carefully qualified statements ("tends to", "often", "in our experiments")
- **Engineering-focused**: Practical implementation over theoretical discussion
- **Balanced**: Acknowledges unsolved problems and limitations

---

## 幽默/Character

The framework itself is quite serious. No prominent humor elements.
Occasional dry observations about "AI slop" patterns in evaluator criteria.

---

## Certainty Expression

**Typical patterns**:
- "We found" / "In our experiments" / "Our observation was"
- Hedged claims: "tends to", "often", "sometimes"
- Clear distinction between established practice and open questions
- Explicit about what remains unsolved ("behaviour harness is hardest problem")
